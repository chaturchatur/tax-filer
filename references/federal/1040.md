# Form 1040: U.S. Individual Income Tax Return

## Overview

Every individual filing a federal return uses Form 1040. All schedules and supporting forms feed into this form. Process this form last.

## Filing Status (top of form)

Check one box matching `profile.json` filing_status. See `filing-status.md` if user is unsure.

## Line-by-Line Instructions

### Income Section

#### Line 1a: Wages, salaries, tips
- **Source**: W-2 Box 1 (sum all W-2s)
- **Computation**: Sum of all `w2-*.json` -> `boxes.1_wages`
- **Notes**: Includes tips, bonuses, taxable fringe benefits. Does NOT include self-employment income (that goes through Schedule C -> Schedule 1).

#### Line 1z: Total from adding lines 1a through 1h
- **Source**: Sum of Lines 1a-1h
- **Computation**: Usually just Line 1a for simple returns. Add 1b-1h if applicable (household employee wages, tips not on W-2, Medicaid waiver payments, scholarship income, etc.)

#### Line 2a: Tax-exempt interest
- **Source**: 1099-INT Box 8
- **Computation**: Sum of all 1099-INT Box 8 values
- **Notes**: Informational only. Not added to taxable income. Includes municipal bond interest.

#### Line 2b: Taxable interest
- **Source**: 1099-INT Box 1
- **Computation**: Sum of all 1099-INT Box 1 values
- **Notes**: If total > $1,500, Schedule B is required.
- **Triggers**: Schedule B if total > $1,500

#### Line 3a: Qualified dividends
- **Source**: 1099-DIV Box 1b
- **Computation**: Sum of all 1099-DIV Box 1b values
- **Notes**: Taxed at preferential capital gains rates (0%, 15%, or 20%). Subset of ordinary dividends.

#### Line 3b: Ordinary dividends
- **Source**: 1099-DIV Box 1a
- **Computation**: Sum of all 1099-DIV Box 1a values
- **Notes**: If total > $1,500, Schedule B is required.
- **Triggers**: Schedule B if total > $1,500

#### Line 4a: IRA distributions (total)
- **Source**: 1099-R Box 1 (IRA distributions)
- **Computation**: Total IRA distributions received
- **Notes**: Includes both taxable and non-taxable portions.

#### Line 4b: IRA distributions (taxable amount)
- **Source**: 1099-R Box 2a (IRA distributions)
- **Computation**: Taxable portion of IRA distributions
- **Notes**: If Box 2a is blank, the full amount may be taxable. Roth IRA qualified distributions are not taxable.

#### Line 5a: Pensions and annuities (total)
- **Source**: 1099-R Box 1 (pensions/annuities)
- **Computation**: Total pension and annuity payments received

#### Line 5b: Pensions and annuities (taxable amount)
- **Source**: 1099-R Box 2a (pensions/annuities)
- **Computation**: Taxable portion

#### Line 6a: Social Security benefits (total)
- **Source**: SSA-1099 Box 5
- **Computation**: Total Social Security benefits received

#### Line 6b: Social Security benefits (taxable amount)
- **Source**: Computed using provisional income formula
- **Computation**: Up to 85% may be taxable depending on total income. Use the Social Security Benefits Worksheet in IRS instructions.
- **Notes**: If combined income (AGI + nontaxable interest + half of SS) is under $25,000 (single) or $32,000 (MFJ), none is taxable.

#### Line 7: Capital gain or loss
- **Source**: Schedule D Line 21, or Schedule D Line 16 if no Schedule D needed
- **Computation**: Net capital gain or loss from Schedule D
- **Notes**: Capital loss limited to $3,000 per year ($1,500 if MFS). Excess carries forward.
- **Triggers**: Schedule D

#### Line 8: Other income from Schedule 1
- **Source**: Schedule 1 Line 10
- **Computation**: Total additional income from Schedule 1
- **Notes**: Includes business income, rental, unemployment, alimony received, etc.

#### Line 9: Total income
- **Source**: Sum of lines 1z, 2b, 3b, 4b, 5b, 6b, 7, 8
- **Computation**: Add all income lines together

### Adjustments Section

#### Line 10: Adjustments to income from Schedule 1
- **Source**: Schedule 1 Line 26
- **Computation**: Total adjustments (IRA deduction, student loan interest, SE tax deduction, HSA deduction, etc.)

#### Line 11: Adjusted Gross Income (AGI)
- **Source**: Line 9 - Line 10
- **Computation**: Total income minus adjustments
- **Notes**: AGI is the most important number on the return. It determines eligibility for many credits and deductions.

### Deductions Section

#### Line 12: Standard deduction or itemized deductions
- **Source**: `form-index.json` -> `standard_deduction_2025` OR Schedule A Line 17
- **Computation**: Use the larger of standard deduction or itemized deductions
- **Notes**: Standard deduction for 2025: Single $15,700 / MFJ $31,400 / HOH $23,500. Add $1,600 (single/HOH) or $1,300 (MFJ) if 65+ or blind.

#### Line 13: Qualified business income deduction
- **Source**: Form 8995 or 8995-A
- **Computation**: Up to 20% of qualified business income (from Schedule C, etc.)
- **Notes**: Complex calculation. Only applicable if user has qualified business income. Subject to income limits.

#### Line 14: Total deductions
- **Source**: Line 12 + Line 13
- **Computation**: Sum of deductions

#### Line 15: Taxable income
- **Source**: Line 11 - Line 14
- **Computation**: max(0, AGI - total deductions)
- **Notes**: Cannot be negative. If deductions exceed AGI, taxable income is 0.

### Tax and Credits Section

#### Line 16: Tax
- **Source**: Tax table or tax computation worksheet
- **Computation**: Look up tax based on taxable income (Line 15) and filing status using the brackets in `form-index.json` -> `tax_brackets_2025`
- **Notes**: If qualified dividends or net capital gains exist, use the Qualified Dividends and Capital Gain Tax Worksheet for potentially lower rates.

#### Line 17: Amount from Schedule 2, Part I, Line 4
- **Source**: Schedule 2 Line 4
- **Computation**: Additional taxes (AMT, excess premium tax credit repayment)

#### Line 18: Sum of Lines 16 and 17
- **Computation**: Line 16 + Line 17

#### Line 19: Child tax credit / credit for other dependents from Schedule 8812
- **Source**: Form 8812
- **Computation**: Up to $2,000 per qualifying child under 17. $500 for other dependents.

#### Line 20: Amount from Schedule 3, Line 8
- **Source**: Schedule 3 Line 8
- **Computation**: Nonrefundable credits (education, retirement savings, etc.)

#### Line 21: Sum of Lines 19 and 20
- **Computation**: Total nonrefundable credits

#### Line 22: Subtract Line 21 from Line 18
- **Computation**: max(0, Line 18 - Line 21)
- **Notes**: Credits can reduce tax to 0 but nonrefundable credits can't go below 0.

#### Line 23: Other taxes from Schedule 2
- **Source**: Schedule 2 Part II Line 21
- **Computation**: Self-employment tax, unreported SS/Medicare tax, additional tax on IRAs, etc.

#### Line 24: Total tax
- **Source**: Line 22 + Line 23
- **Computation**: Sum of tax after credits plus other taxes

### Payments Section

#### Line 25a: W-2 federal tax withheld
- **Source**: W-2 Box 2 (sum all W-2s)
- **Computation**: Sum of all `w2-*.json` -> `boxes.2_federal_tax_withheld`

#### Line 25b: 1099 federal tax withheld
- **Source**: Various 1099 forms, Box 4
- **Computation**: Sum of all federal tax withheld from 1099s

#### Line 25c: Other withholding
- **Source**: W-2G, other forms showing withholding
- **Computation**: Sum of other withholding

#### Line 25d: Total federal tax withheld
- **Computation**: 25a + 25b + 25c

#### Line 26: Estimated tax payments
- **Source**: `profile.json` -> estimated payments
- **Computation**: Sum of all estimated tax payments made during the year

#### Line 27: Earned Income Credit (EIC)
- **Source**: Computed based on income, filing status, and qualifying children
- **Computation**: Use the EIC table. Income limits for 2025 vary by number of children.
- **Notes**: Refundable credit. Can result in a refund even if no tax owed.

#### Line 28: Additional child tax credit
- **Source**: Form 8812
- **Computation**: Refundable portion of child tax credit

#### Line 29: American Opportunity Credit
- **Source**: Form 8863 Line 8
- **Computation**: Up to 40% of the AOC is refundable (max $1,000)

#### Line 31: Amount from Schedule 3, Line 15
- **Source**: Schedule 3 Part II
- **Computation**: Other refundable credits and payments

#### Line 33: Total payments
- **Computation**: Sum of lines 25d through 32

### Refund or Amount Owed

#### Line 34: Overpayment (refund)
- **Computation**: If Line 33 > Line 24: Line 33 - Line 24
- **Notes**: This is the refund amount.

#### Line 37: Amount owed
- **Computation**: If Line 24 > Line 33: Line 24 - Line 33
- **Notes**: This is what the user owes. Can pay electronically via IRS Direct Pay (free) or by check with the return.

#### Line 38: Estimated tax penalty
- **Notes**: May apply if user owed > $1,000 and didn't pay enough through withholding or estimated payments. Form 2210 calculates the penalty.

## Common Mistakes

1. Forgetting to include state tax refund as income on Schedule 1 (if itemized last year)
2. Double-counting HSA employer contributions
3. Not using the Qualified Dividends worksheet when applicable (results in overpaying)
4. Forgetting to claim the refundable portion of education credits (Line 29)
5. Not including all W-2s (if multiple jobs)
6. Claiming standard deduction when itemizing would save more
7. Forgetting estimated tax payments made during the year
