# Form 8889: Health Savings Accounts (HSAs)

## Overview

Report HSA contributions, compute the deduction, and report distributions. Must file if you made or received HSA contributions or took distributions.

## Eligibility

Must have a High Deductible Health Plan (HDHP):
- **2025 minimums (verify)**: Self-only $1,650 deductible / Family $3,300 deductible
- **2025 max out-of-pocket**: Self-only $8,300 / Family $16,600

## Contribution Limits (2025, verify)

- Self-only coverage: $4,300
- Family coverage: $8,550
- Catch-up (age 55+): additional $1,000

## Line-by-Line Instructions

### Part I: HSA Contributions and Deductions

#### Line 2: HSA contributions you made (not employer)
- **Source**: User-provided or HSA account statements
- **Notes**: Does NOT include employer contributions (those are in W-2 Box 12 Code W)

#### Line 6: HSA deduction
- **Computation**: Lesser of contributions made or contribution limit, minus employer contributions
- **Flows to**: Schedule 1 Line 13

#### Line 9: Employer contributions (including through cafeteria plan)
- **Source**: W-2 Box 12 Code W
- **Notes**: Already excluded from W-2 Box 1 wages. Don't double-count.

### Part II: HSA Distributions

#### Line 14a: Total distributions
- **Source**: 1099-SA Box 1

#### Line 14c: Qualified medical expenses paid with HSA
- **Source**: User-provided
- **Notes**: Only distributions for qualified medical expenses are tax-free

#### Line 15: Taxable HSA distributions
- **Computation**: Line 14a - Line 14c
- **Notes**: Taxable amount is included in income. Plus 20% penalty if under 65 and not for qualified medical expenses.

## Flows To

- **Schedule 1 Line 13**: HSA deduction (Part I)
- **Schedule 1 Line 9** (if applicable): Taxable HSA distributions
- **Schedule 2**: 20% penalty on non-qualified distributions (if applicable)

## Common Mistakes

1. Contributing more than the annual limit
2. Double-counting employer contributions (W-2 Code W) as personal contributions
3. Not pro-rating the limit if HDHP coverage was not for the full year
4. Taking distributions for non-medical expenses before age 65 (20% penalty)
