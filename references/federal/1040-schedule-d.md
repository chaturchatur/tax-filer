# Schedule D: Capital Gains and Losses

## Overview

Report sales of stocks, bonds, crypto, real estate, and other capital assets. Determines net capital gain or loss.

## Who Files This

- Sold stocks, bonds, mutual funds, ETFs
- Sold cryptocurrency
- Sold real estate (other than primary residence excluded under Section 121)
- Received capital gain distributions (1099-DIV Box 2a)

## Key Concepts

- **Short-term**: held 1 year or less -> taxed as ordinary income
- **Long-term**: held more than 1 year -> taxed at preferential rates (0%, 15%, or 20%)
- **Cost basis**: what you paid for the asset (including commissions)
- **Proceeds**: what you sold it for

## Line-by-Line Instructions

### Part I: Short-Term Capital Gains and Losses

#### Line 1a: Short-term totals from Form 8949 Box A
- **Source**: Form 8949 (basis reported to IRS, short-term)
- **Computation**: Total proceeds - total cost basis for short-term covered transactions

#### Line 1b: Short-term totals from Form 8949 Box B
- **Source**: Form 8949 (basis NOT reported to IRS, short-term)

#### Line 1c: Short-term totals from Form 8949 Box C
- **Source**: Form 8949 (no 1099-B received, short-term)

#### Line 7: Net short-term capital gain or loss
- **Computation**: Sum of lines 1a through 6

### Part II: Long-Term Capital Gains and Losses

#### Line 8a: Long-term totals from Form 8949 Box D
- **Source**: Form 8949 (basis reported, long-term)

#### Line 8b: Long-term totals from Form 8949 Box E
- **Source**: Form 8949 (basis NOT reported, long-term)

#### Line 8c: Long-term totals from Form 8949 Box F
- **Source**: Form 8949 (no 1099-B, long-term)

#### Line 13: Capital gain distributions
- **Source**: 1099-DIV Box 2a
- **Notes**: These are always long-term, even if you held the fund short-term

#### Line 15: Net long-term capital gain or loss
- **Computation**: Sum of lines 8a through 14

### Part III: Summary

#### Line 16: Combine lines 7 and 15
- **Computation**: Net short-term + net long-term
- **Notes**: If positive, you have a net capital gain. If negative, you have a net capital loss.

#### Line 21: Net capital gain or loss
- **Computation**: If net loss, limited to -$3,000 (-$1,500 if MFS)
- **Notes**: Excess loss carries forward to next year. Track carryover amount.

## Flows To

- **1040 Line 7**: Net capital gain or loss (Line 21)
- If net gain and qualified dividends exist, use the Qualified Dividends and Capital Gain Tax Worksheet to compute tax at preferential rates

## Form 8949

Schedule D summarizes Form 8949. For each sale:
- Description of property
- Date acquired
- Date sold
- Proceeds
- Cost basis
- Gain or loss

Brokerages provide 1099-B with this info. For covered transactions (basis reported to IRS), you can sometimes enter totals directly on Schedule D without a separate 8949.

## Capital Gains Tax Rates (2025)

| Rate | Single | MFJ | HOH |
|------|--------|-----|-----|
| 0% | Up to $48,350 | Up to $96,700 | Up to $64,750 |
| 15% | $48,350-$533,400 | $96,700-$600,050 | $64,750-$566,700 |
| 20% | Over $533,400 | Over $600,050 | Over $566,700 |

These rates apply to net long-term capital gains and qualified dividends.
