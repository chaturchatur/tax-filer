# Filing Status Decision Tree

Ask these questions in order to determine the correct filing status.

## 1. Were you married on December 31 of the tax year?

**Yes** -> go to question 2
**No** -> go to question 3

## 2. Married: how do you want to file?

**Married Filing Jointly (MFJ)**
- Both spouses report all income and deductions on one return
- Generally most advantageous for married couples
- Both spouses are jointly liable for the tax
- Choose this if: both spouses agree, and combined return results in lower tax

**Married Filing Separately (MFS)**
- Each spouse files their own return
- Usually results in higher combined tax
- Choose this if: spouses don't want joint liability, one spouse has high medical expenses or miscellaneous deductions, or estranged/separated

**Notes**: If one spouse died during the tax year, you can still file jointly for that year.

## 3. Unmarried: do you have a dependent?

**Yes, with qualifying child/dependent** -> consider Head of Household (question 4)
**No** -> **Single**

## 4. Head of Household eligibility

Must meet ALL of these:
- Unmarried (or considered unmarried) on last day of tax year
- Paid more than half the cost of keeping up a home for the year
- A qualifying person lived with you for more than half the year

**Qualifying persons**: child, stepchild, foster child, sibling, parent (parent doesn't need to live with you if you pay >50% of their home costs)

If all met -> **Head of Household** (better brackets and higher standard deduction than Single)
If not all met -> **Single**

## 5. Qualifying Surviving Spouse

If your spouse died in one of the two prior tax years AND you have a dependent child:
- You can use this status for up to 2 years after spouse's death
- Same brackets and standard deduction as MFJ
- In the year of death, file MFJ

## Summary Table

| Status | Standard Deduction (2025) | Best for |
|--------|--------------------------|----------|
| Single | $15,700 | Unmarried, no dependents |
| MFJ | $31,400 | Most married couples |
| MFS | $15,700 | Specific situations (liability, deductions) |
| HOH | $23,500 | Unmarried with qualifying dependent |
| QSS | $31,400 | Recent widow(er) with dependent child |

## Common Mistakes

- Filing Single when eligible for Head of Household (leaves money on the table)
- Filing MFS without checking if MFJ would be cheaper
- Claiming HOH without meeting the "paid more than half the cost" requirement
- Not realizing you can be "considered unmarried" if separated for last 6 months of year
