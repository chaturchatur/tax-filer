# Form 8863: Education Credits

## Overview

Claim the American Opportunity Credit (AOC) or Lifetime Learning Credit (LLC) for qualified education expenses.

## Two Credits (choose one per student)

### American Opportunity Credit (AOC)
- **Max credit**: $2,500 per student
- **Computation**: 100% of first $2,000 + 25% of next $2,000 of qualified expenses
- **Refundable**: 40% is refundable (up to $1,000)
- **Eligibility**: First 4 years of postsecondary education, at least half-time enrollment
- **Limit**: Can only claim for 4 tax years per student
- **MAGI phase-out (2025, verify)**: Single $80,000-$90,000, MFJ $160,000-$180,000

### Lifetime Learning Credit (LLC)
- **Max credit**: $2,000 per return (not per student)
- **Computation**: 20% of up to $10,000 of qualified expenses
- **Refundable**: No (nonrefundable only)
- **Eligibility**: Any postsecondary education, no enrollment requirement, no year limit
- **MAGI phase-out (2025, verify)**: Single $80,000-$90,000, MFJ $160,000-$180,000

### Which to Choose

- If first 4 years of undergrad and at least half-time: **AOC** (always better -- higher max, partially refundable)
- If grad school, part-time, or already claimed AOC for 4 years: **LLC**
- Cannot claim both for the same student in the same year

## Qualified Expenses

- Tuition and required fees
- Course materials (books, supplies, equipment) -- AOC only
- Does NOT include: room and board, transportation, insurance, medical

**Net qualified expenses** = 1098-T Box 1 - Box 5 (scholarships)

If Box 5 > Box 1, the excess scholarship may be taxable income (report on 1040 Line 1, Schedule 1).

## Line-by-Line Instructions

### Part I: Refundable American Opportunity Credit

#### Line 1: Student name, SSN, institution
- **Source**: 1098-T

#### Line 2: Adjusted qualified expenses
- **Source**: 1098-T Box 1 - Box 5
- **Computation**: Net qualified expenses for this student

#### Line 3: Tentative credit
- **Computation**: If expenses >= $4,000: $2,500. If $2,000-$4,000: $2,000 + (expenses - $2,000) x 0.25. If < $2,000: expenses x 1.00

#### Line 7: Tentative AOC (after MAGI reduction)
- **Computation**: Apply phase-out if MAGI exceeds threshold

#### Line 8: Refundable portion
- **Computation**: Line 7 x 0.40 (max $1,000)
- **Flows to**: 1040 Line 29

### Part II: Nonrefundable Education Credits

#### Line 9: Nonrefundable AOC
- **Computation**: Line 7 - Line 8 (the remaining 60%)

#### Line 10-18: Lifetime Learning Credit computation
- Similar flow: qualified expenses x 20%, then apply MAGI phase-out

#### Line 19: Total nonrefundable education credits
- **Computation**: Line 9 + Line 18 (LLC)
- **Flows to**: Schedule 3 Line 2

## Flows To

- **1040 Line 29**: Refundable AOC (Line 8)
- **Schedule 3 Line 2**: Nonrefundable education credits (Line 19)

## Common Mistakes

1. Claiming AOC for a 5th year (limit is 4 tax years)
2. Not reducing expenses by scholarships (Box 5)
3. Including room and board as qualified expenses
4. Claiming the credit when MAGI is over the phase-out
5. Not claiming the refundable portion (some people leave $1,000 on the table)
6. Claiming both AOC and LLC for the same student
